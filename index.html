Dashboard Key / Legend

Core Metrics

CE (Case Equivalents)Standard unit of measurement. 1 CE = one 12-bottle case of 750ml wine. All volume figures are expressed in CE for consistency across bottles, cases, and mixed orders.
13W CETotal case equivalents depleted (sold through to retail/accounts) over the most recent 13-week period. This is the primary volume metric.
4W VelocityCase equivalents depleted in the most recent 4-week window. Used to gauge current momentum vs. the full 13-week picture.
YoY (Year-over-Year)Comparison of the current 13-week period vs. the same 13-week period one year ago. Shown as a percentage change. "New" means no comparable prior-year data exists.
MoM (Month-over-Month)Direction indicator comparing the most recent 4 weeks vs. the prior 4 weeks. Shows whether volume is accelerating or decelerating.
Revenue (Rev)Net dollar value of depletions or sales. On the Depletions tab this is distributor sell-through revenue. On the Revenue & Sales tab this is MT's QuickBooks revenue.
Avg Rev/CaseAverage revenue per case, calculated from case-only transactions (excludes individual bottle sales). Represents what MT receives per case sold.
ROS (Rate of Sale)Rate of sale per account or per placement. Measures how fast product moves at the retail level.

Inventory & Supply

On Hand (OH)Current inventory held at the distributor, measured in case equivalents.
DOH (Days on Hand)Number of days current inventory would last at the current daily depletion rate. DOH = On Hand / Daily Rate of Sales.
Daily RateAverage daily depletion in CE, calculated from the most recent 90-day period.
Projected OrderEstimated reorder quantity in CE based on projected 90-day sales minus current on-hand inventory. Negative means overstocked.
Inventory Status Healthy 60-120 DOH
Low Under 60 DOH - reorder soon
Overstocked 120-365 DOH
Critical Over 365 DOH or zero velocity
ConsistencyPercentage of weeks (out of 13) where a distributor had at least some depletion. 1.0 = sold every week. 0.23 = sold in only 3 of 13 weeks. Measures reliability of sell-through.

Placements & Accounts

Effective PlacementsNumber of retail accounts that have purchased at least once in the measured period. Different from distribution points (which counts locations stocking the product).
New PlacementsAccounts that purchased in the last 30 days but had NOT purchased in the prior 90 days. These are either brand-new accounts or reactivated accounts.
Lost PlacementsAccounts that purchased in the prior 60-day window but have NOT purchased in the most recent 30 days. These are at risk of churning. "Days Since Last Sale" shows how long since their last order.
Net PlacementsNew placements minus lost placements. Positive = growing account base. Negative = losing more accounts than gaining.
Account Trend Accelerating - Monthly CE is increasing over the 4-month period
Decelerating - Monthly CE is decreasing
Stable - Relatively flat month-to-month
Reorder CycleAverage number of days between purchases for a given account. Calculated from the 4-month rolling period. Used to predict when an account is "overdue" for their next order.

Revenue & Sales (QuickBooks)

Revenue SourcesRevenue & Sales data comes from QuickBooks (MT's accounting system), NOT from distributor depletion reports. This represents what MT actually receives, not what distributors sell through to retailers.
Bottles SoldTotal individual units sold across all transaction types (cases, individual bottles, samples). Includes all channels.
Cases (CE)Bottles Sold divided by 12. Converts total units to case equivalents for comparison with depletion data.
Customer Channels Distributors - Sales to distribution partners (Fedway, Breakthru, Republic, etc.)
Direct to Trade - Off Premise - Direct sales to retail stores, wine shops
Direct to Trade - On Premise - Direct sales to restaurants, bars, hotels
Website / DTC (FBM) - Online sales, direct-to-consumer, fulfilled by merchant
Promo - Promotional orders (samples, events, tastings)
Operations - Internal/operational transactions
State Filter NoteRevenue data is company-wide and does NOT filter by state. The QuickBooks data does not have state-level breakdowns. Depletion, inventory, placement, and account tabs DO filter by market.

Dashboard Tips

State/Market FilterUse the dropdown or pill buttons at the top to filter all data by state. Click "All Markets" to reset. Revenue & Sales tab always shows company-wide data regardless of filter.
Click-to-Filter ChartsOn the Inventory, Depletions, and Placements tabs, you can click chart segments to filter the table below. For example, click "Critical" on the inventory doughnut to see only critical distributors. Click again or use the X badge to clear the filter.
Pricing Not ReportedSome distributors (marked with "pricing not reported") do not share their sell-through pricing. Their revenue and rev/case columns show dashes to avoid displaying misleading numbers. Their volume data (CE, velocity, etc.) is still accurate.
AI Analysis CardsBlue cards at the bottom of each tab provide automated insights and recommended actions based on the data. These recalculate when you change the state filter.
Data FreshnessCheck the "Data as of" date in the header. The dashboard is rebuilt from 7 Excel exports: 6 VIP depletion reports and 1 QuickBooks sales detail export.
